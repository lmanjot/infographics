The Supporting Factors: A Healthy Foundation

PRP stimulates follicles, but they need an optimal environment and the right nutrients to grow. We test these values to ensure your body is ready to respond.

Ferritin (Iron): Iron is a cofactor for ribonucleotide reductase, an enzyme essential for DNA synthesis. The rapidly dividing cells in the hair matrix have a high iron requirement. Low ferritin levels (iron stores) impair this proliferation and can lead to hair loss. It also contributes to anemia and reduces oxygen supply to the follicle.

TSH & Cortisol: Thyroid hormones (regulated by TSH) are crucial modulators of cellular metabolism in the hair follicle. Imbalances can shorten the anagen phase. High cortisol (stress) is a potent trigger of premature catagen phase (transition phase), leading to telogen effluvium (hair loss).

Vitamin D* & Zinc*: The vitamin D receptor (VDR) is crucial for activating the anagen phase in new follicles. Zinc is an important cofactor for hundreds of enzymes, including those necessary for the synthesis of keratin, the primary structural protein of hair.

CRP & Blood Count: CRP is a marker for systemic inflammation that can create a pro-inflammatory microenvironment around the follicle. The blood count (especially hemoglobin) is crucial for assessing oxygen transport capacity, which is essential for the high metabolic demands of growing hair.
