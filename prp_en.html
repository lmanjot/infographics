PRP: The Bio-Stimulator

A detailed analysis of cellular mechanisms

Direct Effects

Growth Factor Release

After injection, platelets are activated and release a high concentration of growth factors such as PDGF, VEGF, EGF, and FGF. These molecules are the primary signaling agents that initiate the regeneration process.

Dermal Papilla (DP) Stimulation

Growth factors bind to receptors on dermal papilla cells, the "brain" of the follicle. This stimulates cell proliferation and reactivates important signaling pathways for hair growth, particularly the ERK and Akt pathways.

Bulge Region Stem Cell Activation

Signals from the activated dermal papilla reach stem cells in the bulge region. This awakens dormant follicles and causes stem cells to proliferate and form a new hair shaft.

Indirect Effects

Angiogenesis Promotion

Growth factors like VEGF promote the formation of new blood vessels around the follicle. This improved vascularization increases the supply of oxygen and nutrients, which is essential for robust hair growth.

Anagen Phase Extension

By activating anti-apoptotic signaling pathways (e.g., through Bcl-2 protein), PRP helps prevent programmed cell death in the follicle. This extends the active growth phase and leads to longer, thicker hair.

Signaling Pathway Modulation

PRP influences important signaling pathways such as the Wnt/β-catenin pathway, which is crucial for initiating the anagen phase. By activating this pathway, PRP helps effectively "reset" and normalize the hair cycle.
